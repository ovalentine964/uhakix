# CONSTITUTION OF KENYA 2010
## Reference Document for UHAKIX Citizens Rights Module

**Source:** Kenya Law Reform Commission - Official Constitution

**Important:** This is a reference document for the UHAKIX system to provide citizens with constitutional knowledge. All citizens have the right to know their rights under this Constitution.

## BILL OF RIGHTS (Chapter 4)

### ARTICLE 19 — Rights and Fundamental Freedoms
1. The Bill of Rights is an integral part of Kenya's democratic state and is the framework for social, economic and cultural policies.
2. The purpose of recognising and protecting human rights and fundamental freedoms is to preserve the dignity of individuals and communities and to promote social justice and the realisation of the potential of all human beings.

### ARTICLE 20 — Application of Bill of Rights
1. The Bill of Rights applies to all law and binds all State organs and all persons.
2. Every person shall enjoy the rights and fundamental freedoms in the Bill of Rights to the greatest extent consistent with the nature of the right or fundamental freedom.

### ARTICLE 21 — Implementation of Rights and Fundamental Freedoms
1. The State and every State organ is to observe, respect, protect, promote and fulfil the rights and fundamental freedoms in the Bill of Rights.
2. The State shall take legislative, policy and other measures, including the setting of standards, to achieve the progressive realisation of the rights guaranteed under Article 43.

### ARTICLE 22 — Enforcement of Bill of Rights
Every person has the right to institute court proceedings claiming that a right or fundamental freedom in the Bill of Rights has been denied, violated or infringed, or is threatened.

### ARTICLE 23 — Authority of courts in respect of rights and fundamental freedoms
Courts have full authority to make orders and grant relief to ensure the protection of rights.

### KEY RIGHTS EVERY KENYAN MUST KNOW:

| Article | Right | What It Means |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| Art 26 | Right to Life | Every person has the right to life |
| Art 27 | Equality & Freedom from Discrimination | No discrimination on any basis |
| Art 28 | Human Dignity | Every person's dignity must be respected |
| Art 29 | Freedom & Security | Freedom from violence, torture, arbitrary detention |
| Art 31 | Privacy | Privacy of home, communications, personal information |
| Art 33 | Freedom of Expression | Right to seek, receive, and impart information |
| Art 34 | Freedom of Media | Media freedom and independence |
| Art 35 | Access to Information | Every citizen has the right to access information held by the State |
| Art 36 | Freedom of Association | Right to form and join associations |
| Art 37 | Assembly, Demonstration, Petition | Every person has the right to assemble, demonstrate, picket, present petitions |
| Art 38 | Political Rights | Every citizen is free to make political choices, form political parties, vote in elections |
| Art 39 | Freedom of Movement & Residence | Every person has the right to freedom of movement and residence anywhere in Kenya |
| Art 40 | Protection of Right to Property | Every person has the right to acquire and own property |
| Art 41 | Labour Relations | Fair labour practices, fair remuneration, safe working environment |
| Art 42 | Clean & Healthy Environment | Every person has the right to a clean and healthy environment |
| Art 43 | Economic & Social Rights | Every person has the right to health care, education, accessible housing, food & water, social security |
| Art 44 | Language & Culture | Every person has the right to use their language and participate in cultural life |
| Art 46 | Consumer Rights | Consumers have the right to fair business practices, accurate information, compensation for harm |
| Art 47 | Fair Administrative Action | Every person has the right to administrative action that is expeditious, efficient, lawful, reasonable and procedurally fair |
